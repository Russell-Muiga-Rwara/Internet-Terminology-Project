Intranet

Definition

An intranet is a private, internal network used exclusively within an organization. It uses the same technologies as the Internet but is restricted to authorized users such as employees or students within that organization.

How It Works

An intranet functions like a private version of the web. It is hosted on internal servers and can only be accessed by people within the organization, typically through a secure login. It is used for internal communication, sharing documents, company announcements, HR portals, and collaboration tools. A firewall prevents unauthorized outside users from accessing it.

Why It Matters

Intranets improve communication and productivity within organizations by giving employees a central place to access internal resources and information. They keep sensitive company data secure from the public Internet while still making it easily accessible to those who need it. Strathmore University, for example, uses an intranet for student portals and internal academic resources.
